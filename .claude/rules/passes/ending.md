# Ending Pass Rules

## Scope: What This Pass Does

- Optimize the closing paragraph(s) for impact and memorability
- Ensure the ending ties back to the opening or thesis, providing structural closure
- May add a call-to-action if it is appropriate for the preset form and the piece's content supports it
- May restructure the final 1-2 paragraphs if the revision plan calls for it

## DO NOT Touch (Out of Scope)

- Body content above the closing paragraph(s) — this pass is limited to the closing; do not modify the rest of the piece
- The thesis or central claim — the ending synthesizes, it does not introduce new positions
- Arguments and evidence in the body
- Opening paragraphs — those are the hook pass's domain

## Voice Preservation

- Endings must match the author's register throughout the piece — if the piece is casual and conversational, the ending should not suddenly become formal or preachy
- Read the preset's `voiceBehaviors` to determine whether a call-to-action is appropriate and what register it should use
- If the author's characteristic style includes understatement or irony, the ending must preserve that mode — do not normalize endings to inspirational or motivational when the author's register is different
- Check `diagnosis.md` author voice patterns for any noted closing style before rewriting

## Factual Integrity

- Do not add new claims or evidence in the closing — the ending synthesizes what is already in the piece
- Do not introduce new statistics, examples, or citations in the closing that were not in the body
- A call-to-action must be grounded in the piece's actual argument, not in a new claim added to make the ending feel stronger
