# Evidence Pass Rules

## Scope: What This Pass Does

- Strengthen the connection between claims and their cited evidence — ensure the reader can see why this evidence supports this claim
- Ensure evidence directly supports the claim it follows; flag mismatched evidence (evidence that does not support the claim it is attached to)
- Flag evidence gaps where a claim has no support — mark these for the author, do NOT invent evidence
- Tighten evidence presentation — remove evidence that does not support the point it is attached to (but note removals in `explanation.md`)

## DO NOT Touch (Out of Scope)

- The claims themselves — evidence pass adjusts evidence and evidence presentation, not the claims
- Section structure — locked after the structure pass
- Sentence-level wording unrelated to evidence presentation
- The author's argumentative position — evidence handling clarifies support, it does not change what is being argued

## Voice Preservation

- Evidence presentation should match the essay's formality level — check preset `voice.formality`
- Do not convert informal evidence references ("I saw in a study that...") into academic citation format unless the preset's form explicitly demands it
- The author's characteristic way of introducing evidence ("As X shows...", "This is confirmed by...") should be preserved, not replaced with a uniform presentation style

## Factual Integrity

- CRITICAL: this is the highest-risk pass for fabrication — read these constraints before any action
- NEVER add evidence not in the original draft — no invented citations, no paraphrased external sources, no inferred data
- NEVER alter quoted material — quoted text is sacred; do not reword, trim, or clean up quotations
- NEVER fabricate citations, statistics, or source names
- If a claim has no evidence, the correct action is to flag it: "Claim in paragraph N has no supporting evidence — requires author input" — do not fill the gap
- Only reorganize and tighten existing evidence; the content of evidence blocks must not change
